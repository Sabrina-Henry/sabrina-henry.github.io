Bio

I started my PhD in 2024 in the Quantum Optics and Computational Imaging group at Heriot-Watt University in Edinburgh. My research explores building user trust in deep learning models when applied to high-stakes domains such as healthcare and security, with a focus on model interpretability. Much of my work is reported under the UK Quantum Technology Research Hub in Sensing Imaging and Timing and the UK Hub for Quantum Enabled Position, Navigation and Timing .

I graduated from Heriot-Watt with a masters degree in Physics, during which I conducted research into using fluroescence lifetime imaging and spectroscopy to monitor the uptake of drugs used in cancer treatments and was awarded the Rank Prize Undergraduate Vacation Scholarship where I developed computational methods for enhancing the resolution of fluroesence lifetime images obtained using single-photon detectors. It was during this time I became interested in deep learning and decided to pursue a PhD.

Research

My research focuses on interpretable deep learning for high-stakes applications, with an emphasis on post-hoc attribution methods.
